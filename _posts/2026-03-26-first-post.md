HEARING: WHAT DOES IT MEAN?
___

Think of your ears as a window. When the 
window is open, you can hear just about 
anything, without 'trying' to hear, like 
birds chirping, people talking nearby, and 
even the wind. Sound flows in naturally. 

Here's another analogy: Imagine you are 
standing in a stadium. In all directions, 
you hear people cheering, booing, laughing, 
and maybe even crying. Without thinking or even 
straining to hear, the sounds travel into ears 
to get processed and transmitted to the brain. 
The brain, then does its job of recognizing those 
sounds and picking out what is relevant to you, and 
helps filter out unnecessary noise for concentration. 

___

HEARING LOSS: THE DEFINITION

___

Hearing loss occurs when there is damage in the hearing system. 
There are different type of degrees of loss, but I'll
go over that next time. When hearing decreases, it can feel 
like the volume of all sounds has turned down. It feels
like silence but not exactly. Many times, conversations 
are difficult to understand, sound distorted or fuzzy. 
You may hear an airplane in the sky fly over your city, but
you also may not hear whispers.

___

WHY IS HEARING SO IMPORTANT? 

___

Hearing loss does, in fact, affect a person's quality of life.
What does that mean? It means, for the person with the loss, it 
becomes so tiring to strain their ears and use their brain, that
they start to isolate themselves. Imagine trying to listen to your 
favorite TV show with the volume barely audible. It is definite that 
you may have to lean in to keep up but it is also known that ... the difficulty ...
